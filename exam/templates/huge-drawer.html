Thanks to your help, my mother in law went in a cabinet-buying frenzy and bought a 10000 mm tall cabinet (yes, 10 m, she has high ceilings, she lives in a warehouse) to store her legos.

She can buy as many drawers of length 21 mm, 12 mm, 9 mm, and 6 mm as she needs (tiny legos => tiny drawers).

She want to know how many drawers of each size she can use, she wishes to minimize the amount of unused space.

In order to parse the answer, my mother in law needs to see all the drawers she should buy, as integers, on a single line, separated by commas.

So, for example, for a smaller cabinet:

  • Total height: 30
  • Available drawers: 21, 12, 9, 6
one answer could be: 
            21, 9

Hints and tips:

  • Check the ortools example of a knapsack problem.
  • But also read the reference documentation because choosing the wrong algorithm won't let you solve this huge problem. If the computer thinks for more than a handful of seconds, you are doing it wrong.
  • You can try multiple times.
  • Focus on getting the right answer first, and then format it in a way my mother in law can understand
